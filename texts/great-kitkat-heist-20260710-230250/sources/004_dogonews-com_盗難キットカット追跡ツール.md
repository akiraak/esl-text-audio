# The Great KitKat Heist: Factual Details

**Theft Particulars:**
A truck carrying Formula 1 car-shaped KitKat bars disappeared en route from a Nestlé factory in central Italy to Poland. The shipment contained "more than 413,000 KitKat bars" intended as part of a special release marking the 75th anniversary of Formula 1 racing and the 90th anniversary of KitKat.

**Timeline:**
- March 28, 2026: Heist news broke, prompting speculation about an April Fools' prank
- April 2, 2026: Nestlé launched its recovery initiative
- April 10, 2026: No recovery yet reported

**Recovery Method:**
Nestlé introduced the "Stolen KitKat Tracker" on its website. Consumers can "scan the code on the wrapper or enter the 8-digit barcode" to determine if their candy is part of the stolen shipment and learn how to report findings.

**Public Assistance:**
The site explains how to report identified stolen bars. As of the article date, the company has "insist[ed] the heist was real and even asked the public for help."

**Current Status:**
"As of April 10, 2026, however, there is still no sign of the stolen KitKats."
