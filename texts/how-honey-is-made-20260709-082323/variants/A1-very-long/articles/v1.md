# How Bees Make Honey

Bees make honey. This is a small drop, but it has a big story. Bees work together. They visit flowers. They carry sweet water. They change it into honey. This story has five parts: flowers, bees, science, history, and today.

## Bees Visit Flowers

A bee flies to a flower. The flower has sweet water inside. This sweet water is nectar. The bee drinks the nectar. It goes into a special part of the bee's body. This part is like a small bag. The nectar stays in the bag. The bee does not eat the nectar. The bee carries the nectar back to the hive. The hive is the bees' home. Many bees fly to many flowers every day. They visit many kinds of flowers.

## Bees Share the Nectar

The bee returns to the hive. Another bee waits at the door. The two bees touch their mouths. The first bee gives the nectar to the second bee. It is like giving a small gift. The second bee carries the nectar deeper into the hive. Many bees do this job. They pass the nectar from bee to bee.

## The Magic Change

Something amazing happens now. Each bee adds a small drop from her own body. This drop is a natural helper. It changes the nectar. The nectar has one big sugar. Now it becomes two small sugars. The nectar slowly becomes honey. This change happens step by step, bee by bee.

## Wind from Small Wings

The new honey is still very wet. Wet honey does not last long. The bees have a plan. They stand near the honey. They move their wings very fast. Their wings make wind. The wind dries the honey. Now the honey is not wet. It is thick and sweet. The bees close the honey cell. They use wax from their own bodies. The wax is like a small lid. It stops air and water.

## A Busy Bee Family

A hive has many bees. One queen bee lives in the hive. She lays many eggs. Worker bees do many jobs. Young worker bees clean the hive. They also feed baby bees. Older worker bees build the honeycomb. They also carry nectar. The oldest worker bees fly outside. They also protect the hive. Every bee has a job. The bees work as one big team.

## Honey Never Goes Bad

Honey is a very special food. It does not go bad. Three things protect the honey. First, honey is very dry. Germs need water, but honey has very little water. Second, honey is very sour. Sour food stops bad germs. Third, bees add a special drop to the honey. This drop stops germs too. But honey needs one thing: a closed lid. Air or water can enter without a lid. Then the honey can go bad.

## People and Bees, Long Ago

People love honey for a very long time. The story starts about nine thousand years ago. People find wild honey and climb up to get it. Old paintings on rocks show this. In Egypt, long ago, people keep bees in long clay hives. Egypt's kings have a special name: "Bee King." Honey is very important in Egypt. People and bees are friends for a very long time.

## Honey Today

Today, people still love honey. Farmers keep bees in many countries. Every year, the world makes a lot of honey. Africa makes more and more honey every year. But bees do more than make honey. Bees visit flowers and help plants grow. Many fruits and vegetables need bees. Without bees, we do not have enough food. Bees are small, but they are very important.

## One Small Drop

A tiny drop of honey has a long journey. It starts at a flower. It passes from bee to bee. It changes with a magic drop. It dries in the wind. It waits in a closed cell for a long time. People love this drop for nine thousand years. Small bees make a big gift for us.
