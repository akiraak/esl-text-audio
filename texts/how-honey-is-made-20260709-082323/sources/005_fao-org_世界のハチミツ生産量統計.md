# Global Honey Production Statistics

**Total Global Production:**
In 2023, worldwide honey production reached "1 894 thousand tonnes." Africa accounts for approximately 12 percent of this global total, producing "223 000 tonnes annually."

**Regional Growth:**
The article emphasizes that Africa demonstrates the highest growth rate in honey production globally, though specific growth percentages are not provided.

**Leading African Producers:**
Ethiopia ranks as the continent's largest honey producer and tenth globally. Other significant African producers mentioned include Algeria, Angola, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Egypt, Kenya, Morocco, Rwanda, and Tanzania.

**Economic Value of Pollinators:**
The document notes that without pollinators, the world would lose approximately 8 percent of global crop production annually, valued at "around USD 577 billion" economically.

**Pollination Dependency:**
Nearly 90 percent of flowering plants depend on pollinators, as do 75 percent of the world's most important food crops providing fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds.

**Note:** The article does not provide specific figures regarding the number of honey bee colonies worldwide.
