# KitKat Heist Article - Factual Content

**Date of Theft:** Last week (article updated March 29, 2026)

**Location:** Stolen during transit between Italy and Poland in Europe

**Origin & Destination:** The truck departed from a factory in central Italy and was heading to Poland

**Quantity Stolen:**
- 12 tons
- 413,793 individual KitKat units

**Product Details:** New chocolate range of KitKat bars

**How Theft Occurred:** A truck transporting the shipment disappeared while in transit between production and distribution locations

**Current Status:** "The vehicle and its contents remain unaccounted for"

**Nestlé Statements:**
- "A truck transporting 413,793 units of its new chocolate range has been stolen during transit in Europe"
- Warned of potential Easter shortages and that bars "could enter unofficial sales channels across European markets"

**Investigation Details:** "Investigations are ongoing in close collaboration with local authorities and supply chain partners"

**Tracking Tool:** Each bar contains unique batch codes that can be scanned to trace stolen goods. Upon match, instructions direct users to alert KitKat for evidence sharing.
