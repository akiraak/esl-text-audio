# F1 KitKat Theft Article - Factual Extract

**Incident Details:**
A truck carrying 413,793 Formula 1-themed KitKat bars was stolen while en route from central Italy to Poland on March 30, 2026. The shipment weighed approximately 12 tonnes. The exact location along the 776-838 mile route remains undisclosed by authorities.

**Product Information:**
The stolen bars represent KitKat's official Formula 1 partnership (established 2025), coinciding with F1's 75th anniversary and KitKat's 90th year. The range includes milk chocolate shells with crispy cereal and wafer pieces, plus F1 KitKat Chunky (launched January 2026). Each bar carries a unique tracking code.

**Company Response:**
Nestlé stated: "We've always encouraged people to have a break with KitKat. But it seems thieves have taken the message too literally and made a break with more than 12 tonnes of our chocolate."

The company warned the bars could enter unofficial markets and noted that cargo theft represents an escalating criminal issue across Europe, particularly affecting food and pharmaceutical shipments.

**Investigation Status:**
The truck remains unfound. Local authorities are conducting an investigation. Nestlé confirmed overall supply remains unaffected by the loss.
