# How Bees Make Honey

Bees make honey. First, a bee visits a flower. It takes sweet juice from the flower. This juice is nectar. The bee carries the nectar to the hive. Then another bee takes the nectar. This bee adds something from its body. The nectar slowly turns into honey. The bees fan their wings. This dries the honey. Then the bees close it with wax.

Honey with a good seal does not go bad. It has very little water, and it is very sour. These things stop bad germs.

Long ago, people found honey outside. Today, people still eat honey. Bees also help flowers and food grow. A tiny drop of honey has a big story.
