# Honey Bee Social Organization

## Colony Roles

**Queen**: The sole reproductive female in most colony phases, described as having "a longer and plumper abdomen than does the worker." She produces all offspring within the colony.

**Workers**: Non-reproductive females performing all brood care, hive maintenance, and defense. They are the smallest caste and represent the majority of the colony's population.

**Drones**: Male bees with larger heads and thoraxes than females. Their primary function is "passing the colony's genes on to the next generation by mating with queens from other colonies."

## Labor Division Over Worker Lifespan

Workers progress through predictable age-based tasks, a pattern called temporal polyethism:

- **Young bees**: Perform "cleaning brood cells, feeding and tending the brood, and tending to the queen" in the hive's central area
- **Middle-aged bees**: Handle "building comb, receiving nectar and pollen, storing nectar and pollen, processing honey and ventilating"
- **Oldest bees**: Perform "guarding the hive, removing dead bees from the hive, and foraging"

This progression is regulated by juvenile hormone levels, which activate or deactivate glands to match physiological needs for each task. The system demonstrates flexibility—younger bees advance to foraging if foragers die, and older bees revert to brood care if young bees are scarce.
